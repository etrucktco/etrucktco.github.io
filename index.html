Mathematical caveats & honest limitations

  • Linear payback: we use a simple linear approximation ΔCAPEX / avg yearly saving. Real payback uses the cumulative cashflow row in the cashflow table (which respects the growing-savings curve and subsidy timing). The two differ by ±0.5 year typically.
  • No NPV / discounting: all values are nominal. To convert to NPV, apply discount rate d per year: NPV = Σ CFt / (1+d)t. At d=5%, lifetime savings drop ~15%. At d=8%, ~22%.
  • Inflation: not modeled. Diesel and electricity prices are held constant over the contract. In practice diesel typically rises faster than electricity (especially with EU ETS-2 from 2027), so this understates the electric advantage.
  • Battery degradation: we assume linear capacity over the contract. Real LFP cells lose 10-15% over 7 years; affects residual value but not OPEX.
  • Public charging: excluded. If >10% of mileage requires Milence-style charging (€0.46/kWh), the case weakens by €0.5-1.5M per fleet per year.
  • Annualized fleet yield: simple linear (ROI / years), not compound IRR. Compound IRR would be slightly higher because savings come progressively, not lump-sum.
  • Country factor: applies only to "other" OPEX (tolls) and diesel taxation. Energy prices, subsidy rates, and labor differ further across BE/NL/DE/FR but are not modeled here.

Why these numbers are realistic

  • Annual km: 100,000 km/year is typical for heavy-duty regional transport. Long-haul reaches 150,000+ km/year.
  • Gross CAPEX: we use gross (no upfront subsidy deduction). Subsidies appear as tax-deductible OPEX in years 2-4. This matches accounting reality.
  • Maintenance curve: realistic — diesel grows €4.8k → €15.5k/yr over lifetime; electric stays €2.2k → €6.8k/yr (LFP battery + regenerative braking).
  • Tolls: BE/NL/DE heavy goods toll — zero-emission trucks remain exempt until 2031.
  • Public charging: NOT included. Public MCS charging (Milence €0.46/kWh) is not cost-competitive vs diesel. Business case assumes 90%+ depot charging.
  • Residual value: electric truck €30k, infrastructure €0.78M, captured in year 7.
  • Diesel price (pump vs. net): the slider shows the pump price you see at the station. For commercial fleets > 7.5 t, Belgium refunds €0.1913/L excise duty in 2026 (down from €0.1924 in 2025, €0.1935 in 2024 — the scheme is gradually phased out). Our calculations use the net price after refund. NL, DE, FR have different schemes; this demo uses the BE rate as baseline.
  • Subsidy range: EU AFIF caps at 50% of eligible infra costs (70% in outermost regions, for chargers ≥1MW). Stacked with Flemish Ecologiepremie+ (24-32% on trucks), VLIF/EP+ (up to 40% on battery storage), or Dutch SPULA (~20% on public charging) you reach 50-70% combined. Above 70% requires individual state-aid notification.

Sensitivities you can test

  • Move diesel price up: the case improves dramatically (every €0.10/L extra typically adds €3-5M in savings on a medium fleet over the contract period).
  • Move depot power price down: a long-term PPA at €0.14/kWh can add €2-3M+ to the case.
  • Increase subsidy: each 10% subsidy increase typically shifts ROI delta by 8-12 percentage points.
  • Extend the contract length: each extra year compounds the OPEX advantage. LFP batteries support 10+ year lifetimes.
  • Try different truck models: lower CAPEX directly improves ROI.
  • Change annual km: higher mileage means more fuel saved per year — energy-intensive fleets benefit most.
